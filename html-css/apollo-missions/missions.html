Explore the Missions

Here is an overview of all the Apollo Missions. To learn more about a specific mission, click on the mission name.

Apollo 1 (1967)

Apollo 1 was planned to be the first crewed Apollo mission. During a launch rehearsal test, a cabin fire claimed the lives of astronauts Virgil I. Grissom, Edward H. White II, and Roger B. Chaffee, leading NASA to redesign spacecraft safety systems.

Apollo 4 (1967)

Apollo 4 was the first uncrewed test flight of the Saturn V rocket. The mission successfully demonstrated that the powerful rocket could launch and return a spacecraft safely.

Apollo 5 (1968)

Apollo 5 tested the Lunar Module in space for the first time. The mission verified that the spacecraft's engines and systems worked properly for future moon landings.

Apollo 6 (1968)

Apollo 6 was another uncrewed test of the Saturn V rocket. Despite some technical problems, the mission provided valuable data and helped prepare NASA for crewed lunar flights.

Apollo 7 (1968)

Apollo 7 was the first crewed Apollo mission. The crew spent 11 days in Earth orbit testing the Command and Service Module and proving that the spacecraft was ready for lunar missions.

Apollo 8 (1968)

Apollo 8 became the first crewed spacecraft to travel and orbit the Moon. The mission demonstrated that humans could safely reach lunar orbit and return to Earth.

Apollo 9 (1969)

Apollo 9 tested the Lunar Module with astronauts aboard while in Earth orbit. The mission successfully demonstrated docking, seperation, and operation of the lander.

Apollo 10 (1969)

Apollo 10 served as the full dress rehearsal for the Moon landing. The crew traveled to lunar orbit and flew the Lunar Module close to the Moon's surface without landing.

Apollo 11 (1969)

Apollo 11 achieved the first human landing on the Moon. Astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin explored the lunar surface while Michael Collins orbited above.

Apollo 12 (1969)

Apollo 12 completed NASA's second successful Moon landing. The crew landed with remarkable precision and collected scientific samples and equipment from the lunar surface.

Apollo 13 (1970)

Apollo 13 suffered a major onboard explosion while en route to the Moon. Through careful problem-solving by the astronauts and mission controllers, the crew returned safely to Earth.

Apollo 14 (1971)

Apollo 14 carried out NASA's third successful Moon landing. The astronauts conducted scientific experiments and collected lunar sample from the Fra Mauro region.

Apollo 15 (1971)

Apollo 15 was the first mission to use the Lunar Roving Vehicle (LRV). The crew traveled farther across the Moon's surface and greatly expanded scientific exploration.

Apollo 16 (1972)

Apollo 16 landed in the lunar highlands to study a different type of terrain. The mission gathered important geological data and continued extensive rover operations.

Apollo 17 (1972)

Apollo 17 was the final Apollo mission and the last human landing on the Moon. The crew conducted extensive scientific research and set records for time spent on the lunar surface.
